Operational Notes: Tuning Retrieval-Augmented Agents (Part 1)

When we moved our support assistant from a single-shot prompt to a retrieval-augmented loop, p95 latency rose before it fell. This note records what moved the numbers, in order.

1. Chunking dominates recall

512-token windows with 64-token overlap recovered most answers the 1,024-token windows had split across boundaries. Recall@5 went from 0.61 to 0.83 with no model change.

2. Cache the embedding calls, not the completions

A content-addressed embedding cache cut vector-build cost ~40% on re-indexing and removed a latency spike on document updates.

3. Grounding quality is a freshness problem

Most logged "hallucinations" were stale-context: the retriever surfaced an older revision. Pinning retrieval to the latest revision removed most of them.
