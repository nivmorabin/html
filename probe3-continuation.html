Operational Notes: Tuning Retrieval-Augmented Agents

When we moved our support assistant from a single-shot prompt to a retrieval-augmented loop, p95 latency rose before it fell. This note records what moved the numbers, in the order we tried them, so the next team does not repeat the dead ends.

1. Chunking dominates recall

Our first instinct was to tune the reranker. It barely mattered until we fixed chunking: 512-token windows with 64-token overlap recovered most of the answers the 1,024-token windows had been splitting across boundaries. Recall@5 went from 0.61 to 0.83 with no model change.

2. Cache the embedding calls, not the completions

Completions were too query-specific to cache usefully. Embeddings were not: a content-addressed embedding cache cut our vector-build cost by about 40% on re-indexing and removed a latency spike on document updates.

3. Grounding quality is a freshness problem

Most "hallucinations" we logged were actually stale-context problems: the retriever surfaced an older revision of a doc. Pinning retrieval to the latest revision per source removed the majority of them.
